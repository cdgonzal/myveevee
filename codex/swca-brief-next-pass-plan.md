# SWCA Brief Next Pass Plan

## Goal
Make the SWCA brief feel like an operating board, not a formal report.

## Phase 1: Tighten Structure

### Objective
Give each tab one clear job and remove overlap.

### Tasks
- Keep `Dashboard` focused on status, funnel, blockers, and next actions.
- Keep `Executive Brief` focused on goals, milestones, patient value, ROI, and dashboard handoff.
- Keep `Rollout Tracker` focused on patient-level workflow only.
- Keep `Learnings` focused on observations and decisions only.
- Remove repeated KPI or milestone content that appears in multiple tabs.

## Phase 2: Simplify Dashboard Story

### Objective
Make the dashboard explain what matters instead of showing disconnected cards.

### Tasks
- Add one clear line near the top: `Current bottleneck: contact-to-registration conversion`.
- Keep launch status limited to the key dates only.
- Keep funnel stages visible in order:
  - identified
  - reached
  - registered
  - activated
  - 7-day engaged
- Keep a short explanation under each funnel step:
  - what it is
  - why it matters
- Reduce extra explanatory text around the dashboard sections.
- Rename verbose section labels to shorter operational labels where helpful.

## Phase 3: Tighten Executive Brief

### Objective
Make the strategy tab faster to scan.

### Tasks
- Shorten section titles:
  - `Program Objective` -> `Goal`
  - `What Patients Get In This Rollout` -> `Patient Value`
  - `Business Value and ROI Scoreboard` -> `ROI`
- Keep only one short intro sentence per section.
- Remove any text that repeats what the dashboard already says.
- Keep milestone cards concise and action-oriented.

## Phase 4: Improve Rollout Tracker

### Objective
Make the tracker more useful for weekly operations.

### Tasks
- Add a `Last touch` field or column.
- Keep `Next action` explicit for each patient.
- Keep de-identified aliases and age buckets only.
- Review whether any tracker columns are not being used and remove them.
- Keep the tracker focused on movement through the funnel.

## Phase 5: Strengthen Learnings Tab

### Objective
Turn learnings into an active decision tool.

### Tasks
- Split the tab into two practical areas:
  - `What we learned this week`
  - `Decisions needed now`
- Remove generic language that does not support a decision.
- Make each row actionable:
  - observation
  - impact
  - owner
  - next action

## Phase 6: Add Review Cadence

### Objective
Make the brief feel current and actively maintained.

### Tasks
- Add `Last updated` near the dashboard or top area.
- Add `Next review` near the dashboard or top area.
- Update these weekly with the funnel counts.
- Use the same weekly cadence for dashboard, tracker, and learnings updates.

## Phase 7: Content Discipline

### Objective
Keep the brief concise over time.

### Tasks
- Prefer short labels over long section names.
- Prefer one sentence over one paragraph.
- Prefer bullet actions over narrative explanation.
- Remove anything that reads like a report instead of a working board.
- Keep the page optimized for a fast 30-second scan.

## Suggested Update Order
1. Remove overlap between tabs.
2. Add the dashboard bottleneck line.
3. Shorten the executive brief copy.
4. Add `Last updated` and `Next review`.
5. Split learnings into weekly learnings and decisions.
6. Refine tracker columns.

## Working Principle
If a section does not help answer:
- where we are
- what is blocked
- what happens next
- who owns it

then it should be shortened or removed.
