# SWCA Brief Improvement Plan

## Goal
Make the SWCA brief useful to Michael, SWCA leadership, and clinicians by showing whether the pilot is working, what is blocked, and what decision comes next.

## What Is Already Done
- Hidden route and soft PIN gate are live.
- Dashboard, Executive Brief, Rollout Tracker, and Learnings tabs are in place.
- Copy has been shortened and the tracker is de-identified.
- Review cadence and bottleneck callout were added.

## What Is Missing
- A clear baseline for current performance.
- A hard definition of pilot success.
- Clear ownership across VeeVee, Michael, and SWCA staff.
- Workflow impact on staff.
- Clinical and patient value stated in plain terms.
- A stronger business case tied to labor or outcome improvement.
- Risks, assumptions, and decision points.

## Phase 1: Reset The Brief Around Decisions

### Objective
Make each tab support a concrete decision instead of acting like a report section.

### Tasks
- Keep `Dashboard` focused on current status, bottleneck, weekly actions, and next decision.
- Keep `Executive Brief` focused on pilot definition, success criteria, patient value, workflow impact, ROI, and go/no-go logic.
- Keep `Rollout Tracker` focused on patient movement and staff follow-up only.
- Keep `Learnings` focused on observed patterns, open risks, and decisions needed.
- Remove any section that repeats counts or dates already visible elsewhere.

## Phase 2: Add Baseline And Success Criteria

### Objective
Show what SWCA is trying to improve and what counts as a win.

### Tasks
- Add a `Baseline vs Current vs Target` block near the top of the Executive Brief.
- Define pilot success in concrete terms:
  - registration target
  - activation target
  - engagement target
  - staff-efficiency target
  - rollout decision target
- Add one line that answers: `What does success look like by formal launch?`
- Add one line that answers: `What would trigger a reset or rethink?`

## Phase 3: Add Ownership And Workflow Impact

### Objective
Make responsibilities and staff burden visible.

### Tasks
- Add an `Ownership` table:
  - area
  - owner
  - support
  - current status
- Add a `Workflow Impact` section:
  - what staff do today
  - what changes with VeeVee
  - what work should be reduced
  - where handoffs can fail
- Add explicit owners for:
  - outreach
  - registration handoff
  - activation follow-up
  - weekly KPI updates
  - launch decision

## Phase 4: Strengthen Patient And Clinical Value

### Objective
Make the brief credible to physicians and operators, not just admins.

### Tasks
- Rewrite `Patient Value` in plain outcomes, not product language.
- Add a short `Why This Helps Patients` block:
  - easier start
  - clearer next steps
  - better follow-through
  - fewer dropped handoffs
- Add a short `Why This Helps SWCA` block:
  - less manual chasing
  - better visibility
  - stronger follow-up consistency
  - clearer proof of value
- Add a short `Clinical Fit` block that states what this pilot is meant to support and what it is not meant to replace.

## Phase 5: Upgrade The ROI Story

### Objective
Make business value concrete enough for leadership review.

### Tasks
- Replace generic ROI copy with a simple business case.
- Add measures such as:
  - staff time per successful registration
  - time from first outreach to registration
  - registration conversion
  - activation conversion
  - estimated cost per enrolled patient
- Add one `Why this matters financially` note for each top ROI metric.
- Add a small `If this works` section showing what broader rollout could unlock.

## Phase 6: Add Risks, Assumptions, And Decision Points

### Objective
Show what could stall the pilot and when leadership needs to act.

### Tasks
- Add a `Top Risks` section:
  - poor contact data
  - low response rate
  - weak handoff to registration
  - low first-use activation
  - unclear staff ownership
- Add an `Assumptions` section with the few facts the pilot depends on.
- Add a `Decision Points` section:
  - by what date do we expect first registrations
  - what happens if conversion stays flat
  - when do we expand
  - when do we stop or redesign
- Add owners and review dates to each decision point.

## Phase 7: Tighten Dashboard To What Actually Moves

### Objective
Track the few metrics that explain momentum.

### Tasks
- Keep the top dashboard focused on:
  - days to next launch milestone
  - patients reached
  - patients registered
  - patients activated
- Add one short readout line:
  - what is working
  - what is stuck
  - what happens next
- Keep the funnel simple:
  - identified
  - reached
  - registered
  - activated
  - engaged
- Add only the drivers that actually move those numbers:
  - outreach readiness
  - response rate
  - registration completion
  - first-use completion

## Phase 8: Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Objective
Make the brief a living operating tool.

### Tasks
- Add a weekly update rule for the page owner.
- Update dashboard counts once per week.
- Update tracker next actions once per week.
- Update learnings and risks once per week.
- Update `Last updated` and `Next review` every cycle.

## Suggested Implementation Order
1. Remove completed cleanup tasks from the brief roadmap.
2. Add baseline and success criteria.
3. Add ownership and workflow impact.
4. Rewrite patient and clinical value.
5. tighten ROI into a business case.
6. Add risks, assumptions, and decision points.
7. Trim the dashboard to the few metrics that actually matter.

## Working Principle
If a section does not help answer one of these, it should be shortened or removed:
- Are we on track?
- What is blocked?
- Who owns the next move?
- Why does this matter to SWCA?
